# Governance

ShareCab is maintained as a source-available product project.

## Maintainer Authority

Maintainers are responsible for:

- Product direction.
- Repository licensing and brand policy.
- Release decisions.
- Security response.
- Code review and merge decisions.
- Community moderation.

Maintainers may decline contributions that are outside the product roadmap,
increase operational risk, conflict with licensing or brand policy, or weaken
rider, driver, payment, or data safety.

## Decision Making

Most decisions are made by maintainer consensus in issues or pull requests.
Security, legal, commercial, and safety decisions may be handled privately when
public discussion would increase risk.

## Contribution Areas

Public contributions are best suited for:

- Documentation.
- Tests.
- Bug fixes.
- Accessibility.
- Localization.
- Developer tooling.
- Clearly scoped product improvements.

Private or maintainer-only areas may include:

- Production credentials and deployment configuration.
- Fraud rules.
- Sensitive safety operations.
- Commercial licensing terms.
- App-store release credentials.
- Internal analytics and business operations.

## Releases

Maintainers decide when changes ship to the official ShareCab apps and services.
Merged code does not guarantee immediate production release.

## Policy Changes

Maintainers may update governance, licensing, contribution, security, and support
policies as the project matures. Material changes should be documented in pull
requests where practical.
