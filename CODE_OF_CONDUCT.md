# Code of Conduct

ShareCab is built around practical, respectful collaboration. This code applies
to repository issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, community spaces, and
maintainer interactions connected to the project.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful, direct, and constructive.
- Assume good intent, but correct harmful impact when it happens.
- Keep discussions focused on the work and the people affected by it.
- Accept feedback professionally.
- Give credit for ideas, reports, and fixes.
- Be careful with safety, privacy, and security topics.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, stalking, or sustained disruption.
- Discriminatory comments or conduct.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted attention.
- Publishing private information without consent.
- Bad-faith security reports, extortion, or public vulnerability disclosure
  before maintainers have had a reasonable chance to respond.
- Spam, trolling, or repeated off-topic arguments.

## Reporting

Reports can be sent to: anandrahul044@gmail.com

Include:

- What happened.
- Where it happened.
- Who was involved.
- Any links, screenshots, or context that help maintainers understand the issue.

Maintainers will treat reports with discretion and will avoid sharing details
beyond what is needed to review and respond.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take action based on severity and pattern of behavior,
including:

- Private clarification or warning.
- Public moderation.
- Temporary restriction from issues, pull requests, or discussions.
- Permanent ban from project spaces.
- Reporting credible safety or legal threats to the appropriate platform or
  authority.

This policy is inspired by common open-source community conduct practices,
including the Contributor Covenant, but is written specifically for ShareCab.
